title: Someone close to me got scammed. How do I help?
summary: A parent, grandparent or friend lost money online or over the phone. Here is what to do in the first hours to limit the damage, without shaming them, and where to report it together.
situation: Someone dear to me lost money to a scam and I don't know how to help
icon: "🫂"
order: 5
severity: urgent
lastReviewed: "9 July 2026"
steps:
  - "<strong>Don't scold them and don't shame them.</strong> Scammers are professionals; it can happen to anyone. If they feel judged, they will shut down and hide details that matter."
  - "Together, calmly, find out <strong>exactly what happened</strong>: what information they gave, what amounts, to whom, and by what method (card, bank transfer, an installed app)."
  - "If they shared banking details or sent money, <strong>call their bank now</strong> using the official number on the back of the card — block the card and ask about a payment recall."
  - "If they installed a \"remote access\" app (AnyDesk, TeamViewer) or any other app the scammer asked for, <strong>uninstall it</strong> and cut off the access; check the accounts on that phone/computer."
  - "Change the passwords of the affected accounts and turn on two-factor authentication. Gather the evidence (screenshots, bank statement) for the police report."
donts:
  - "Don't let them send more money \"to recover\" what they lost — that is stage two of the scam."
  - "Don't hide the incident out of shame: reporting quickly increases the chances of recovery."
  - "Don't pass the numbers or accounts on to \"recovery firms\" that appear right away — they are often scammers too."
recognize:
  - "<strong>A sudden change in behaviour:</strong> secrecy, unusual payments, a new \"friend/advisor\" they met online."
  - "They received calls or messages \"from the bank/police\" and were rushed into acting."
  - "They talk about a \"safe investment\" or about \"a parcel/prize\" they paid fees for."
report:
  - channel: "The victim's bank"
    detail: "Block the card and ask about a payment recall / chargeback. The official number is on the back of the card."
  - channel: "DNSC"
    detail: "Report the incident to DNSC (Romania's national cybersecurity directorate) and ask for guidance."
    phone: "1911"
    url: "https://dnsc.ro"
  - channel: "The Police"
    detail: "File a complaint together with the Police (Poliția Română), with all the evidence. Go with the person who was scammed."
    url: "https://www.politiaromana.ro"
  - channel: "112"
    detail: "If the scam is happening right now (e.g. they are at the ATM, or on the phone with the scammer)."
    phone: "112"
sources:

**First, breathe — and be on their side.** The most important thing right now is not "how could they fall for it", but "what do we do in the next few hours". Your kindness gets them to tell you everything, and your speed matters most for recovering the money.

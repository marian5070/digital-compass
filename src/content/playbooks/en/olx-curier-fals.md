title: I got a link "to receive my money" on OLX. What do I do?
summary: You're selling on OLX (Romania's biggest classifieds site) and the "buyer" sends you a link from a courier (Fan Courier, Sameday) to "receive" or "confirm" the money. It's a scam. Here's how to recognise it and what to do.
situation: Someone sent me a link so I can "receive" money on OLX
icon: "📦"
order: 8
severity: urgent
lastReviewed: "10 July 2026"
steps:
  - "<strong>Do not open the link and do not enter your card details.</strong> Remember the golden rule: to RECEIVE money you never need your card number, PIN or any code. Only to SEND money."
  - "If you have already typed in your card details, <strong>call your bank now</strong> on the number on the back of your card and ask them to block it."
  - "Keep the conversation <strong>inside the OLX app only</strong> and use the platform's own delivery and payment system, not links received via chat, WhatsApp or SMS."
  - "Report the user and the message in OLX."
  - "If you lost money, call your bank (payment recall) and file a report with the Police (Poliția Română), with your screenshots."
donts:
  - "Do not give your card details, PIN or any code to \"receive\" money — that is never how money is received."
  - "Do not leave OLX for WhatsApp at the request of the \"buyer\" or the \"courier\"."
  - "Do not send a photo of your ID card or your bank card."
recognize:
  - "The \"buyer\" is in a big hurry, pays immediately and insists you receive the money through a <strong>link from a courier</strong>."
  - "The link looks like Fan Courier / Sameday / OLX (Romania's main couriers and classifieds site), but the web address is slightly different (extra letters, hyphens, a different domain)."
  - "You are asked to \"confirm receiving the money\" by entering your <strong>card details</strong> — that is the clear sign of a scam."
  - "The page asks for your card PIN or the code received by SMS. No genuine incoming payment ever asks for these."

**First, breathe.** The trick behind this scam is that it makes you believe you are *receiving* money, so you let your guard down. But the truth is simple: nobody needs your card details to *send* you money. If a link asks for them "so you can get paid", it's a scam — and now you know.

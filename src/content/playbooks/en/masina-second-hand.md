title: I'm buying a second-hand car and I'm asked for a deposit. What do I do?
summary: A car "from Germany" at a good price, but the seller wants a deposit before you see it, through "secure transport". Here's how to recognise the scam and what to do.
situation: I want to buy a car (especially an import) and I'm asked for a deposit
icon: "🚗"
order: 13
severity: important
lastReviewed: "10 July 2026"
steps:
  - "<strong>Don't send any deposit before you see the car in person.</strong> \"Reserve it with a deposit\" for a car you can't see is the classic sign of a scam — often the car doesn't even exist."
  - "Watch out for \"<strong>secure transport</strong>\" or a \"company that holds the money until delivery\" (a kind of \"escrow\"): it's usually a fake page. The real payment happens in person, when the car and its papers are handed over."
  - "Check the car's history (real mileage, damage) through an official service before any payment."
  - "If you've already paid, call your bank and ask for a payment recall, and gather all the evidence."
  - "File a complaint with the Police (Poliția Română) and, if it's a dealer, a complaint with ANPC (Romania's consumer protection authority)."
donts:
  - "Don't pay a deposit or the full price before seeing the car and its papers in real life."
  - "Don't trust the \"transport company\" or the \"escrow\" proposed by the seller themselves."
  - "Don't send money to a different account \"for tax or customs reasons\"."
recognize:
  - "A price well below the market for a sought-after model."
  - "The seller is \"away abroad\", you can't see the car, and they want a deposit by bank transfer + delivery by courier."
  - "They offer you a \"secure payment page\" that guarantees you get your money back if you don't like it."
  - "The photos and documents also appear in other listings (you can check with a reverse image search)."

**First, breathe.** A real car can be seen, touched and checked before you hand over a single penny. The whole scam rests on one thing: convincing you to pay a deposit "to reserve it" before you've seen it. If you can't see the car and its papers, don't send money.

title: An "investment" or crypto — and something's wrong. What do I do?
summary: You were drawn into a "safe" investment or a crypto platform and now you can't withdraw your money, or you're being asked for fees. Here's what to do now and where to report it in Romania.
situation: I put money into an investment/crypto and something's wrong
icon: "💸"
order: 6
severity: urgent
lastReviewed: "9 July 2026"
steps:
  - "<strong>Stop any further payment, now.</strong> Especially if you're being asked for \"fees\" to withdraw your money — no such thing exists, it's the scam continuing."
  - "Gather the evidence: screenshots of the platform, the conversations with the \"consultant\", receipts, transfer details, the website address."
  - "Check whether the company is <strong>authorised by ASF</strong> (Romania's Financial Supervisory Authority). ASF publishes lists of unauthorised entities and warnings."
  - "If you paid by card, call your bank and ask for a <strong>chargeback</strong>; if you sent a bank transfer, ask for a payment recall. As quickly as possible."
  - "Report it to ASF and DNSC (Romania's national cybersecurity directorate) and file a complaint with the Police (Poliția Română), with all the evidence."
donts:
  - "Don't pay \"withdrawal fees\", \"tax\" or an \"account unlock fee\" — they're invented to squeeze out even more money."
  - "Don't install remote access apps (AnyDesk, TeamViewer) requested by the \"broker\" or \"consultant\"."
  - "Don't hand over card details or codes \"so we can process your withdrawal\"."
recognize:
  - "<strong>A \"guaranteed\", high, risk-free return</strong> — it doesn't exist. It's the classic sign of fraud."
  - "Pressure to deposit quickly or deposit more; a dedicated, very friendly \"consultant\"."
  - "<strong>A video of a public figure</strong> (Ion Țiriac, Mugur Isărescu — governor of Romania's National Bank — or a politician) \"recommending\" an investment platform. These are almost always <strong>deepfakes</strong> — these people never endorse investment schemes."
  - "You got there through a TikTok/Facebook ad or a WhatsApp/Telegram group."
  - "<strong>A WhatsApp/Telegram group</strong> with a \"mentor\"/\"analyst\" and lots of enthusiastic \"members\" (often bots). They let you withdraw small amounts at first, to gain your trust, then they block the larger sums."
  - "The platform shows profits growing nicely, but won't let you withdraw."
report:
  - channel: "ASF (Financial Supervisory Authority)"
    detail: "Check whether the company is authorised and report it. ASF keeps lists of unauthorised entities and warnings."
    url: "https://asfromania.ro"
  - channel: "Your bank"
    detail: "Ask for a chargeback (card) or a payment recall (bank transfer). The official number is on the back of your card."
  - channel: "DNSC"
    detail: "Report the fraudulent platform and ask for guidance."
    phone: "1911"
    url: "https://dnsc.ro"
  - channel: "Police"
    detail: "File a complaint for fraud, with all the evidence."
    url: "https://www.politiaromana.ro"

**First, breathe.** If something is "too good to be true", it usually is. You're not stupid for ending up here — these schemes are built by professionals to look real. What matters is what you do now: stop the payments and check.

  - "Don't share your <strong>work password</strong> with anyone — not a colleague, not the \"IT department\" calling you."
  - "Turn on the <strong>\"second step at sign-in\" (2FA)</strong> on your work accounts."
  - "Use only the apps and tools approved by your company; <strong>don't upload client data</strong> to personal services (personal email, personal drive, unapproved apps)."
  - "<strong>Lock your screen</strong> when you step away from your desk, even for a minute."
  - "Client data is confidential — treat it as if it were your own."

When you work with a company's data and its clients' data, you don't need to be a security expert. A few simple habits, done consistently, make almost all the difference. The most important: **your password is yours alone**, and on any mistake or suspicion, **you speak up fast** — you don't hide it.

greenFlags:
  - "<strong>The company's details are visible:</strong> name, CUI (the Romanian company registration number), address and a real contact (phone/email), not just a form."
  - "It has a <strong>clear returns and delivery policy</strong> — in the EU you have the right to return online purchases within 14 days."
  - "You can pay <strong>by card or on delivery</strong>, not only by transfer in advance."
  - "Real reviews, on independent sources (not just screenshots posted by the store itself)."
  - "A correct web address, with <strong>https</strong>, no extra letters or odd hyphens in the domain."
redFlags:
  - "<strong>A price far too low</strong> compared to the market, plus pressure: \"last items\", \"today only\"."
  - "It asks for payment <strong>only by direct bank transfer</strong> to a person, or in crypto."
  - "No company details, no real contact, text in broken Romanian."
  - "A brand-new domain, or it pushes you onto WhatsApp to \"complete the order\"."
  - "Reviews all 5 stars, generic, all posted on the same day."
rules:
  - "Google \"<strong>[store name] + țeapă / review / păreri</strong>\" (Romanian for \"scam\" and \"opinions\") before you pay."
  - "Check the company by its CUI on the official registers (ANAF, the Romanian tax authority / the Trade Register)."
  - "Pay <strong>by card</strong> (you have the right to a chargeback), not by direct transfer to a person."
  - "Don't enter your card details on pages opened from ads — go to the website directly yourself."
  - "At the slightest doubt, don't buy. A serious store doesn't rush you."
